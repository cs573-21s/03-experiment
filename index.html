In this experiment, you will be asked to determine what percentage a smaller value is to a larger value.

There will be three types of visualizations with different amounts of randomized data.

There is no time limit.

We will not record any information except for your response.

When you are ready, click Start to begin.

Please enter the percent value as an integer (1-100) (without percent sign).

Instructions:

Two regions are marked with dots.

Estimate what fraction (AS A PERCENT) the smaller region is to the larger region.

E.g. if smaller region is 50% the size of bigger region, answer with 50.

Instructions:

Two regions are marked with dots.

Estimate what fraction (AS A PERCENT) the lighter region is light to the darker region in terms of darkness.

E.g. if lighter region is 50% as dark than darker region, answer with 50.

Instructions:

Two words are marked with dots.

Estimate what fraction (AS A PERCENT) the smaller word is to the bigger word in terms of size.

E.g. if smaller word is 50% as the size of bigger word, answer with 50.
